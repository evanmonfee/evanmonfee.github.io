The serenity of reading

Listening to the cold rain fall hard as you snuggle under a warm blanket. A cup of hot coffee and a good book; the dewy petrichor fills the air. Isn’t that what they call the bliss of solitude?

One can often catch me with a fine novel, either while travelling, waiting for someone or simply lazing on the couch on a warm Sunday afternoon serenely reposed in afterthoughts.

My passion for books started at a very early age with my subscription to a children’s monthly magazine, Gokulam. What started off as a curiosity, progressed to an eager wait every month. And now, years down the line I have an ample collection of books at my home (much to annoyance of my mom).

I personally prefer descriptive books that invest words to build the scene in your head before playing out the story-line. Reading a good novel, often takes you to a different world – a world where fiction and creativity comes alive. You discover new information because more often than not, good research forms the backbone of a good piece of writing. Be it an entire book or just a paragraph off a magazine, you learn unconsciously word by word, sometimes a bit more about life.

For a person can only have a few experiences through living; the rest he experiences through reading!
